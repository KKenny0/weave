# Shared Frame Selection

Run after the workflow has built its evidence model and before Compose. Workflow files own route-specific lens generation; this protocol owns candidate admission, comparison, hold-out testing, and chapter trace.

## Inputs

- `x`: user-supplied or collected material
- `q`: the user's exact question, decision, and named focus areas
- `E`: route-specific evidence model: Source Briefs and Synthesis evidence, behavior paths, or Source Catalog and map evidence
- candidate lenses generated by the selected workflow
- pre-designated hold-out evidence

Do not choose a frame before `E` is solid. Early intuitions may guide reading, but they cannot become the article spine without passing the same gates as later candidates.

## Candidate Frame Brief

Create one internal brief per candidate:

```markdown
### {candidate name}
- Question answered: {specific q}
- Default view: {what an ordinary summary or inventory would show}
- Frame: {one evidence-backed sentence}
- Operators used: {route-specific lenses}
- Foreground: {evidence and distinctions selected}
- Background or cut: {material intentionally excluded and why}
- Load-bearing components: {smallest necessary set}
- Evidence map: {components -> E}
- Structural consequence: {how chapters or comparisons change}
- Boundary: {condition, counterexample, or gap}
- Hold-out prediction: {what the frame expects before reveal}
```

The brief is internal. Do not paste it into the final article.

## Candidate admission

Discard a candidate that fails any gate:

1. **Evidence fidelity**: every load-bearing component maps to `E`.
2. **Specificity**: the frame cannot move unchanged to an adjacent source, project, or domain.
3. **Selection effect**: it changes at least two of evidence inclusion, exclusion, chapter order, causal explanation, comparison set, or predicted boundary.
4. **Boundary honesty**: it names a real limiting condition, counterexample, or evidence gap.
5. **Distinctness**: it is not a paraphrase of another retained candidate.

Keep 1-4 candidates. This is a cap, not a target. One admitted candidate is enough. If none pass, return to the route's evidence loop instead of lowering the gates.

## Compare candidates

Compare admitted candidates in this order:

1. direct fit to `q` and named focus areas
2. evidence strength and verification status
3. compression: observations explained per load-bearing component
4. generative reach beyond construction evidence
5. cognitive delta from the default view
6. economy under component-removal testing

Do not assign decorative numeric weights. Write a short comparative judgment grounded in the briefs. A losing candidate must remain a plausible article, not a strawman.

When two candidates remain close, select the one with stronger evidence and narrower claims. Record the runner-up only if it would create a materially different article.

## Test the hold-out

Reveal the pre-designated hold-out after selecting `f*`.

- **Pass**: the frame explains it without changing core components.
- **Partial**: it explains part and the existing boundary predicts the miss.
- **Miss**: it fails outside its stated boundary; narrow the claim and report the miss.
- **Retrofit failure**: core components are changed after reveal to claim success. Return to candidate comparison or evidence collection.

The hold-out is a test, not another source to silently absorb.

For evals or audit-sensitive runs, persist the Candidate Frame Brief, hold-out identifiers, and pre-reveal prediction before opening the hold-out. Save them under the run's internal workspace (for example `.weave-frame/pre-reveal.md`) and include the path in the run report. Capture the timestamp from the environment clock rather than inventing or manually estimating it.

Treat requests for a smoke report, full pipeline verification, or complete delivery report as audit-sensitive. Before reveal, verify that the pre-reveal file exists and can be read back. Without that file, do not claim verified chronology or a hold-out pass; report the run as failed or chronology unverified and rerun from before reveal.

If the primary source can only be fetched atomically and that fetch exposes the entire candidate hold-out, do not reuse a section of that source as if it were unseen. For an audit-sensitive run, reserve an independent, unopened supporting source by canonical title/URL as the hold-out, persist the prediction, then open it. If no independent hold-out can be collected, chronology remains unverified and the executable run gate must fail.

The persisted pre-reveal artifact has a strict allowlist:

- environment timestamp;
- workflow and non-personal topic label;
- hold-out identifier and pre-reveal prediction;
- Candidate Frame Brief fields defined above;
- provisional selection and comparative judgment.

Persist nothing else. In particular, exclude the Context Envelope under any name, Capability Manifest, host identity, context source categories, memory facts, user baseline or preference, personal constraints, raw `q`, Source Briefs, Source Catalog, Synthesis Pack, and Impact Brief. When `Question answered` would repeat personal context, rewrite it as an impersonal research question such as “which subsystem should be built first”; keep the original `q` only in working context. Inspect the artifact against this allowlist before revealing the hold-out, and remove any prohibited field.

Use this content test on **every line**, including prose inside otherwise allowed fields:

- it may explain why a frame fits the evidence or the impersonal research topic;
- it must not explain why a frame fits the user, their team or project, their current choice, stated leaning, goal, constraint, history, role, or remembered context;
- it must not contain phrases such as “user's decision”, “user context”, “team choosing”, “my preference”, or paraphrases of those ideas;
- the topic may be `workflow and agent architecture`; it may not be `the user's team deciding whether to start with a workflow`.

After writing, read the file back from disk and perform that line-level scrub before opening the hold-out. If any personal or contextual rationale remains, delete it and restart the audit-sensitive run from before reveal. A field name being allowlisted does not make prohibited content inside that field safe.

When tool transcripts are available, identify the later fetch or read event that reveals the hold-out. The final article must still exclude internal artifacts. A retrospective delivery claim or self-authored timestamp alone is not proof of ordering; if no access log or transcript is available, mark chronology `unverified` while preserving the prediction artifact.

## Build the Frame Decision

Record internally:

- selected frame
- why it won
- close alternative, when material
- admission failures and merged paraphrases
- hold-out result
- final boundary
- user's focus coverage

The decision must be reproducible from `E`; aesthetic preference is not enough.

## Map chapters

Before drafting, map each chapter to:

- one selected-frame component;
- evidence used;
- role: establish, explain, contrast, test, bound, or apply.

Delete chapters that do not serve `f*`. If a named focus area cannot map, return to evidence collection or report the limitation. Do not keep a generic background chapter only because the old workflow template had one.

## Compose invariants

- The selected frame controls article order, not source order or repository order.
- Counterevidence and uncertainty stay visible.
- The final article contains no Candidate Frame Brief or internal ranking table.
- A close alternative may appear in the delivery report, not as a second competing article spine.
- Voice Pass changes expression, not the selected frame or evidence weighting.

## Failure handling

| Failure | Action |
|---|---|
| Evidence too thin for any candidate | Return to Collect or route-specific analysis |
| Candidates are paraphrases | Merge them and keep one |
| Candidate does not alter chapter map | Discard it as decorative |
| User focus conflicts with strongest evidence | Answer the focus with explicit limits; do not invent support |
| Hold-out breaks all candidates | Re-open evidence model and generate new candidates |
| Selection remains tied | Choose stronger evidence and narrower claim; report close alternative |
